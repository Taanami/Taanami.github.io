# Clarkson scores 24 points in a single game, Philippines defeats China for their first victory 
 
The game between Philippines and China in the ongoing FIBA World Cup for the 17-32 ranking matches was intense. In the first quarter, both teams were evenly matched, with Li Kai'er and Zhang Zhenlin helping China secure an advantage. The score was tied at 16:16. In the second quarter, Zhou Qi suffered a spinal ligament injury and was ruled out for the rest of the game. However, both teams remained close in score, with China leading by one point at halftime, 40:39.

In the third quarter, Philippines launched a comeback under the offensive efforts of Dwight Ramos and Jordan Clarkson. They also managed to block the attacks of Hu Jinqiu and others on the defensive end, taking the lead.

Clarkson made four consecutive three-pointers and scored 24 points in a single quarter, helping Philippines extend their lead to over 20 points. They entered the final quarter with a score of 73:51.

In the final quarter, Philippines maintained their lead and kept the point difference at around 20 points. They ultimately defeated China 96:75, securing their first victory of the tournament. Jordan Clarkson was the standout player for Philippines, scoring 34 points in total, with 24 points in a single quarter. Dwight Ramos contributed 11 points, 5 rebounds, and 4 assists, while Rhenz Abando had 14 points and 5 rebounds. Kyle Anderson performed well for China, scoring 17 points, 9 rebounds, and 5 assists. Zhang Zhenlin contributed 13 points, 3 rebounds, and 2 assists, Hu Jinqiu had 10 points and 6 rebounds, Zhao Rui scored 12 points and made 4 assists, and Hu Mingxuan scored 12 points and had 2 rebounds.
